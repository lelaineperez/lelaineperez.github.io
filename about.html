About Me

I began my journey as a Computer Science major, exploring technology and systems, but I quickly realized I wanted a career that combined creativity with structure. That led me to project management, where I can use my technical understanding to support creative teams while fueling the imaginative industries I love.

My dream is to work in creative industries, specifically games, toys, and film. Spaces where storytelling, play, and innovation come together. Project management in these industries allows me to bridge the gap between creativity and execution, keeping projects on track without stifling the vision of the team.

I thrive on wearing many hats: coordinating timelines, managing documentation, facilitating communication, and stepping in wherever the project needs support. I enjoy learning new tools, adapting workflows, and continuously refining processes to help teams work efficiently while keeping creativity at the forefront.

Currently, I’m building hands-on experience managing collaborative projects, understanding production pipelines, and learning how creative teams operate at different stages of development. My goal is to grow as a project manager in spaces where technology, storytelling, and play converge to create meaningful experiences.

How I Work as a Project Manager

  • Translating creative ideas into actionable plans
  • Keeping timelines, tasks, and deliverables organized
  • Supporting clear communication across creative and technical teams
  • Adapting processes to fit the project and team needs
  • Learning new tools and workflows quickly in fast-moving environments

My Project Management Style

I see project management as a support role first. My approach focuses on creating structure that empowers creative teams rather than limiting them. I value flexibility, communication, and collaboration, and I’m most effective when I can help teams stay aligned while adapting to changing project needs.

What I’m Currently Building Toward

  • Production pipelines in games and creative media
  • Cross-functional collaboration in creative teams
  • Tools and processes that support creative production
